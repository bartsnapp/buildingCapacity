The width of each rectangle is \dfrac{b-a}{n} because the interval of length b-a is being subdivided into n equal sized rectangles

Thus the abscissa of the left endpoint of the i^{th} rectangle is a+\dfrac{b-a}{n} \cdot i, and so the height of that rectangle is f(a+\dfrac{b-a}{n} \cdot i)

So the area of the i^{th} rectangle is f(a+\dfrac{b-a}{n} \cdot i) \cdot \dfrac{b-a}{n}

So the sum of all n rectangles is \displaystyle\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n} f(a+\dfrac{b-a}{n} \cdot i) \cdot \dfrac{b-a}{n}

Thus in this particular case, R_n = \displaystyle\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n} \left[ A(L+\dfrac{U-L}{n} \cdot i) + B\right] \cdot \dfrac{U-L}{n}

= \dfrac{U-L}{n} \displaystyle\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n} \left[ A(L+\dfrac{U-L}{n} \cdot i) + B\right] because \dfrac{U-L}{n} is a constant, and so can be factored out
