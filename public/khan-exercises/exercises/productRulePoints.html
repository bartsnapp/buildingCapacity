Suppose that:

f(X) = Y1
f'(X) = M1
g(X) = Y2
g'(X) = M2

What is \left.\dfrac{d}{dx}(f(x)\cdot g(x))\right|_{x=X} ?

Y1*M2+Y2*M1

The product rule says (f \cdot g)'(x) = f(x) \cdot g'(x) + g(x) \cdot f'(x)

Plug in x = X

(f \cdot g)'(X) = f(X) \cdot g'(X) + g(X) \cdot f'(X)

(f \cdot g)'(X) = (Y1) \cdot (M2) + (Y2) \cdot (M1)

(f \cdot g)'(X) = Y1*M2+Y2*M1
