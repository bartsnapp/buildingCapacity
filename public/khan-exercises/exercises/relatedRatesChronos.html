Chronos is letting the sands of time slip through his fingers. The sand accumulates in a conical pile whose radius is P times its height. The height of the cone is changing at a rate of R miles per second at the instant that the cone is H miles high.

What rate is the sand leaving his hands at that instant, in cubic miles per second?

Math.PI*P*P*H*H*R

Let h be the height of the cone, and V be the volume of the sand.

Picture

Known Rate: \left. \dfrac{dh}{dt}\right|_{h = H} = R

Unknown Rate: \left.\dfrac{dV}{dt}\right|_{h = H}

Introduction: I will first write an equation relating h and V using solid geometry. I will then implicitly differentiate this equation with respect to time to obtain an equation relating the rates \dfrac{dV}{dt} and \dfrac{dh}{dt}. Finally I will use the information given in the problem to solve for \left.\dfrac{dV}{dt}\right|_{h = H}.

The volume of a cone is one third the area of the base times the height. In this case, the base is a circle of radius Ph. So the area of the base is \pi(Ph)^2. The height is h. So the volume of the cone is \dfrac{1}{3} \cdot \pi(Ph)^2 \cdot h, or in other words V = \dfrac{P*P\pi}{3} \cdot h^3

Differentiating with respect to time, we obtain \dfrac{dV}{dt} = \dfrac{d}{dt}\left(\dfrac{P*P\pi}{3} \cdot h^3\right)

\dfrac{dV}{dt} = \dfrac{P*P\pi}{3}\dfrac{d}{dh}\left( h^3\right) \cdot \dfrac{dh}{dt}, by the chain rule.

\dfrac{dV}{dt} = P*P\pi h^2\cdot \dfrac{dh}{dt}

At the time of interest, h = H and \left. \dfrac{dh}{dt}\right|_{h = H} = R.

Thus \left.\dfrac{dV}{dt}\right|_{h = H} = P*P\pi\cdot H^2\cdot R

Thus the sand is slipping from Chronos' mighty fingers at a rate of P*P*H*H*R\pi cubic miles per second, at the time that the pile is H miles high.
