Coordinates are listed as (\color{blue}{abscissa},\color{green}{ordinate}).

So, for ( \color{blue}{X}, \color{green}{Y} ) our abscissa is X and our ordinate is Y.

The abscissa tells us which vertical line the point is on and the ordinate tells us which horizontal line the point is on.

Since our abscissa is equal to X, the point must be on the vertical line which goes through X on the horizontal axis.
