According to Fowler's Law of Hepatocircumloqutionary Displacement, the concentration of a drug being delivered by an IV drip is A(t) = Rt^Ne^{-At}, where t is the number of minutes since the IV began, and R is the rate of infusion in ml/min. It is quite important to know the maximum concentration the drug will reach. Assume that you are infusing this drug at a rate of R ml/min.

What is the maximum value of the concentration of the drug?

R*Math.pow(N/(A*Math.E),N)

For this rate of infusion, we have A(t) = Rt^Ne^{-At}

Differentiating, we have A'(t) = R\cdotNt^{N-1}e^{-At}-R\cdot At^Ne^{-At}

Setting this equal to zero, R\cdotNt^{N-1}e^{-At}-R\cdot At^Ne^{-At}=0

Nt^{N-1}-At^N=0

N-At=0

t = displayMode(N/A)

The derivative goes from being positive to being negative through this root, and so because it is the only critical point, this must be the location of the absolute maximum value for A.

Thus the maximum concentration of drug is R\cdot \left(displayMode(N/A)\right)^Ne^{-N}
