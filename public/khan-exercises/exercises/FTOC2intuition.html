Geometrically, we see that f(L) \cdot h \approx intVAL

Thus f(L) \approx \dfrac{intVAL}{h} = fVAL

Note: Rephrasing this, we are really saying that f(x) \approx \dfrac{1}{h}\displaystyle\int_{x}^{x+h} f(t)dt = \dfrac{A(x+h)-A(x)}{h} \approx A'(x), where A(x) = \displaystyle\int_{a}^{x} f(t)dt. This suggests the fundamental theorem of calculus.
