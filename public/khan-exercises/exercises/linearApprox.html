Given that f(INITIALX) = INITIALY, and f'(INITIALX) = SLOPE, approximate f(FINALX).

APPROX

Remember that f'(INITIALX) is the slope of the best linear approximation to f near x = INITIALX. How much have your x-values changed? How much should your y-values change as a result?

The slope of a line is, by definition, the factor by which changes in inputs are scaled to give the resulting change in outputs

In symbols, \Delta y = m(\Delta x), where m is the slope of the line.

In this case the slope is f'(INITIALX) = SLOPE and \Delta x = DELTAX

Thus the change in ordinates of the tangent line is \Delta y = SLOPE(DELTAX) = DELTAY

f(INITIALX) = INITIALY and \Delta y = DELTAY, so f(FINALX) \approx INITIALY + DELTAY = APPROX
